02 How it works

One pipeline, four moves

Everything BrainCue does is the same engine cycle. Modes just tune the thresholds — how eager it is, what it grounds in, and where the answer goes.

i.

Listen

System-audio loopback hears your calls; the microphone covers the room. Everything is transcribed live, on your key.

ii.

Decide

Trigger policies watch for the moment worth answering — a question aimed at you, an action item, a summon. Quiet by default.

iii.

Ground

On-device retrieval pulls the relevant lines from your résumé, notes, and approved memories — only that context is sent out.

iv.

Respond

A cited answer streams into the screen-share-invisible Cue Card — or is spoken aloud, stopping the moment you talk over it.

03 One engine, many modes

Modes are presets, not separate products

Each mode is a configuration over the same engine — so what it learns in one, it keeps in all.

Interview Copilot Shipped

You're the candidate

Detects the interviewer's questions and streams grounded answer cues into the Cue Card — retellable live as key points, an explanation, or a STAR story.

Practice Shipped

You're rehearsing

A mock interviewer asks out loud with a real voice, or a sparring drill coaches every spoken answer — then reports on how you did.

Meeting Copilot Labs

You're a participant

Sits in quietly and surfaces context, unanswered questions, action items, and decisions — only above a confidence threshold you set.

Companion Labs

You're working or thinking

An ambient presence with memory: flags tasks, offers context, recalls what you saved — behind deterministic gates, quiet hours, and a hard spend cap.

Interviewer Assist Coming soon

You're the one asking

Suggests questions and follow-ups, tracks competency coverage as the interview runs, and drafts the evaluation afterwards.

Tutor Coming soon

You're learning something

Voice dialogue and drills grounded in whatever material you give it — notes, a textbook chapter, a spec.

04 Why BrainCue

Things a cloud assistant structurally can't do

🎙️ Hears the real conversation

System-audio loopback puts it inside your actual calls and meetings; a microphone covers in-person. It flags the moment worth responding to, as it happens.

🪟 Invisible to screen sharing

The Cue Card is always-on-top for you and excluded from screen capture and recording for everyone else — OS-level window affinity, not a trick.

💡 Grounded in your documents

Answers are retrieved from your résumé, job descriptions, and notes by on-device search — and it says when it doesn't know instead of inventing.

🗣️ A voice of its own

Push-to-talk from anywhere: ask by voice and hear the answer back. Start talking over it and it stops to listen.

🧠 Memory you approve

It can remember across sessions, but nothing is kept silently — memories are proposed, and only ones you approve are ever recalled. Correct or forget any of them.

🔒 Local-first, bring your own key

Data lives in a local database. Your API key is encrypted by the OS keychain and never leaves the main process — the UI only ever learns whether one exists.

⚠️ Use only where AI assistance is permitted. Your data stays on your machine; only the retrieved context and the current moment of conversation are sent to your AI provider.

07 Built in the open

Every PR gets a scorecard, not a shrug

BrainCue runs a published, automated evaluation pipeline on every pull request — and pays deliberate attention to the work most projects undervalue.

📋 A published standard

Instant intake gates, a secret scan, coverage of your changed lines, and a rubric-pinned AI review across eight dimensions. Every scorecard embeds the hash of the rubric that scored it — the rules can't move silently.

🤝 Feedback that's free to act on

The bot never closes PRs and never files "changes requested" — a failed check is a fix window, not a rejection. When a maintainer approves, the merge is automatic.

💰 A priced backlog

Work starts from maintainer-labeled bounty issues — features, tests, docs, performance, security — difficulty-tiered from starter to advanced. BrainCue is also preparing for listing on Bittensor's GitTensor subnet, where merged PRs earn contributors TAO.
