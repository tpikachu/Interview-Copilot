# 1.5.2 — 2026-07-15

**Privacy Mode: the verified fix for windows appearing in screen shares.**

## Fixed

- **BrainCue no longer stays visible in a screen share after you interact with
  it.** Ground-truth testing (a separate capture process using the same Windows
  capture technology as Zoom/Meet/Teams, driven by real OS-level clicks) showed
  that on some Windows 11 builds the compositor silently stops honoring the
  capture exclusion — after clicking the window (even a click on an
  already-focused window), dragging it, a z-order change, and occasionally
  **spontaneously, with no interaction at all** — while Windows still reports
  the exclusion as active. Previously, some of these drops had no event BrainCue
  was listening to, so the window stayed visible in your share **indefinitely**
  (until the next click happened to heal it).

  BrainCue now heals every drop within milliseconds: it re-asserts the exclusion
  synchronously inside the native window messages for activation, clicks, drags,
  and window-position changes, and a fast watchdog (every 50 ms) catches the
  spontaneous drops. Re-asserting is invisible to capture (verified across
  hundreds of re-calls at 55 fps), so this has no visual cost. Measured result:
  interactions that previously left the app readable in a share now show at
  most ~1–3 frames (under 0.1 s) in the worst case — usually nothing.

- **Dropdown menus no longer leak into screen shares.** Native dropdown lists
  (the Cue Card's Type selector and the settings selects) open as *separate OS
  popup windows* that never inherit the app's capture exclusion — so the open
  list was visible to your share even while the app itself was hidden. All Cue
  Card dropdowns are now in-window popovers that are hidden together with the
  window (verified: opening them creates no OS popup window at all).

- `scripts/verify-privacy-capture.mjs` now really toggles Privacy Mode for its
  before/after captures (it was silently calling a settings key that doesn't
  exist).

**After updating, do a 10-second check:** share your screen in a meeting app,
then click the Cue Card a few times, drag it, and open the Type dropdown —
confirm none of it appears in the shared view.
