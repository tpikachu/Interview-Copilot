# 1.5.0 — 2026-07-14

This release makes practice **compound**: every sparring drill is now saved, scored,
and charted — so you can watch yourself get better — and every answer the copilot
writes is tuned to be spoken aloud on the first read.

## Added

- **Practice Loop.** Sparring drills are no longer throwaway. Every spoken answer’s
  coaching — the 1–5 rating, strengths, improvements, and the resume-grounded tip —
  is saved as it happens, and each drill lands in **Reports** like any session:
  - A new **Practice** card at the top of Reports: your overall average, a
    per-drill trend line, and per-competency scores (leadership, ownership,
    technical depth, …) so you can see exactly what to drill next.
  - Each answer is auto-tagged with the **competency** the question probed.
  - Drill reports are assembled from the coaching you already received — no extra
    API cost.

- **Likely follow-up.** After an answer finishes streaming in the Cue Card, the
  copilot predicts the follow-up your interviewer is most likely to ask next —
  specific to what your answer left open (that metric you claimed, the tradeoff you
  skipped). It appears as a quiet note under the answer, and never slows the answer
  itself down.

- **Self-healing transcription.** If the live transcription connection drops
  mid-interview, BrainCue now reconnects itself with smart backoff — a subtle
  “reconnecting audio…” pill shows in the Cue Card while it recovers. No more
  stop-and-resume at the worst possible moment.

## Improved

- **Answers written to be spoken.** The answer prompts were rebuilt around one idea:
  you *read this out loud*. Short one-breath sentences, numbers rounded the way
  people say them, paragraph breaks where you’d pause, and citations placed so they
  never interrupt your flow. **Story** answers now follow a true spoken arc — hook,
  stakes, what you did, the result — and **Explanation** answers open by actually
  answering, then land on a closing line.

- **Honesty on Linux.** Privacy Mode can’t hide windows from screen capture on
  Linux — the app now says so plainly in Settings and the Cue Card instead of
  quietly promising invisibility.

## Under the hood

- A whole-schema tune-up in one migration: practice-coaching storage, session kinds,
  removal of long-dead answer-metadata columns, and a rebuild that fixes foreign-key
  delete behavior from v1.1 (deleting an interview now cleans up its data exactly as
  designed) — verified against real databases before shipping.
- Every change was adversarially reviewed before merge (7 findings confirmed and
  fixed, including a race that could write a practice answer into the wrong drill).
- The E2E harness launch bug and the long-standing “transient rollup build error”
  were root-caused and fixed. The unit-test suite grows to 175.
